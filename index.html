Eye Irrigation Procedure

A clinical guide for cleansing, soothing, and protecting the eyes

Procedure for Eye Irrigation

Before Procedure

  • Assess cause of eye irritation or chemical exposure.
  • Check baseline visual acuity.
  • Explain the procedure and obtain consent.
  • Position the patient with the affected eye lower.
  • Wear gloves and maintain aseptic technique.

During Procedure

  • Start irrigation from **inner canthus → outer canthus**.
  • Maintain continuous, gentle flow.
  • Ask patient to blink frequently.
  • Monitor for discomfort, dizziness, or nausea.
  • Flush until debris or chemical residue is cleared.

After Procedure

  • Reassess visual acuity and eye condition.
  • Dry the area with sterile swabs.
  • Check for persistent redness, swelling, or discharge.
  • Apply eye medication/dressing if prescribed.

Assessment for Eye Irrigation Procedure

Pre-Procedure Assessment

  • Cause of irritation (chemical, foreign body, infection).
  • Redness, swelling, tearing, discharge.
  • Pain level (0–10 scale).
  • Visual acuity comparison.

During Procedure Assessment

  • Flow direction inner → outer canthus.
  • Patient comfort level.
  • Reduction in debris/chemicals.
  • Signs of dizziness or nausea.

Post-Procedure Assessment

  • Remaining redness, swelling, or discharge.
  • Pain relief.
  • Improved visual clarity.
  • Complications like infection or blurred vision.
